IT Support Communication Simulation – Branching Scenario with xAPI

A video-based branching simulation that assesses business English and emotional intelligence in real time. Learners navigate a live IT support call while every decision is tracked to an LRS, revealing communication patterns traditional tests miss. H5P lets me build interactive scenarios without complex code. xAPI tracks each choice so I can see exactly where learners struggle and improve the training.

01 - The problem

Traditional Business English tests measure knowledge, not judgement under pressure

IT professionals at B1–B2 level possess strong technical skills but underperform in client-facing and internal communication depending on the situation and level of pressure. Five senior IT professionals (70+ years combined experience) revealed that they freeze in stand‑ups, escalate too aggressively, or miss emotional cues. A multiple‑choice quiz cannot measure whether someone would sound dismissive to a frustrated client - the gap is real‑time judgement, not vocabulary.

Target audience

  • Working tech professionals – developers, BI consultants, project managers (25–45 yrs)
  • Remote work environment – daily client calls, video stand‑ups, Slack/Teams comms
  • B1–B2 English level – functional but anxious under social pressure
  • Assessment need to be quick and efficient due to time constraints

"For some of the professionals I interviewed, even something as simple as a support request felt like a test. It wasn't just about what you know - it was about how you treat people under pressure. A thoughtful response can work in your favor. A poor one can escalate things in seconds."

Research foundation (one sentence reference)

This project builds on the same five senior IT interviews described in the Needs Analysis case study. The core insight from that research: high technical competence, but performance collapses under social pressure. Where the Needs Analysis focused on identifying gaps, this case study shows how a branching simulation can assess real‑time judgement – specifically the patterns of freezing, over‑escalating, and missing emotional cues.

Design

Branching scenario with emotional consequences

Four stages: Opening, Clarifying, Troubleshooting, Closing. 25 video clips with 3 emotional variations (warm/neutral/cold) based on learner choices. Each decision scores 1–3 points (consultative vs functional vs reactive). The client’s face and tone shift in real time - the consequence is visceral, not just a feedback box. Vidnoz AI generated fairly realistic video clips quickly (Synthesia would have been ideal but is a premium video generator).

Develop

H5P + custom Netlify proxy + xAPI LRS

Built with H5P BranchingScenario, deployed standalone outside LMS (custom wrapper). H5P removes the need for complex coding while still enabling rich interactivity. Implemented xAPI tracking via Netlify serverless proxy to avoid CORS issues. xAPI captures every single choice across branches – unlike SCORM which only records completion. Privacy modal captures name/email with POPIA/GDPR consent. Every choice - opening response, clarifying question, troubleshooting step, closing line - is sent to an LRS for analysis and to assist in my scoring (data is used for a personalised report sent to the student).

03 - Key design decisions
Decision 1

xAPI over SCORM for tracking

SCORM tracks only completion and final scores. xAPI captures every choice across branches. But H5P's dynamic scoring can misrepresent understanding. So I capture student choices directly and apply my own rubric - for far more accurate insight.

Decision 2

Video variations for emotional consequence

Most branching scenarios use text feedback. Here, the client’s face and tone shift to warm/neutral/cold based on learner choices. Learners see the real‑time cost of a dismissive phrase.

Decision 3

Privacy modal with explicit POPIA/GDPR consent

No tracking occurs without consent: learners provide their name and email only after reading a clear privacy notice, because legal compliance builds trust - though I'd like to explore anonymous alternatives, the LRS currently requires an email address.

05 - Outcomes

The simulation is ready for piloting. Once testing is complete, outcomes will include: average scores by stage, most frequent wrong answers, and correlation between communication patterns and final performance. For now, the key output is a fully functional, xAPI‑tracked branching scenario that captures every learner choice – not just a completion flag.

06 - Reflection

What worked

The video variations made emotional consequences visceral – learners see the client’s face shift from warm to cold based on their word choice, something no text‑based feedback can replicate. xAPI tracking captured every decision across all four stages, revealing exactly where learners struggle (most commonly in the Clarifying stage). The privacy modal with explicit POPIA/GDPR consent turned compliance into a trust signal. And grounding the entire simulation in real interview data ensured the scenarios felt authentic, not like generic textbook exercises.

What I'd do differently

While the H5P Branching Scenario worked for the prototype, a bespoke branching exercise would give me full control over scoring logic, video preloading, and xAPI statement design – with cleaner, more reliable tracking. I could also submit every learner choice directly to the LRS without relying on H5P’s quirks. However, building a custom solution from scratch takes significant development time. I've learned to only pursue that level of complexity if the training program proves it needs it – for example, after pilot data shows clear demand for advanced analytics.
