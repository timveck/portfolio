Executive Communication Training: BLUF + Botpress AI Agent

A senior project manager with 19 years of experience kept freezing in interviews. I designed a 5‑lesson executive module starting with Headline‑First (BLUF) and engineered an AI coach inside Botpress using a single, deeply optimized system prompt. The agent gives instant, structured feedback across five vectors: scenario classification, headline assessment, tone analysis, clarity score, and a BLUF rewrite. Botpress lets learners practice anytime and get consistent feedback without waiting for a human coach.

01 - The problem

High competence, limited ability to talk about work at executive level

A senior project manager with 19 years of experience consistently underperformed in interviews and executive conversations. Her own words: “I’m so good, but I’ve never gotten a job because I just couldn’t get my interview right.” She could execute flawlessly, but when asked to explain her process or defend a decision, she circled, over‑explained, and lost her audience. Standard Business English courses didn’t help - they teach vocabulary, not performance architecture. This was not a language problem. It was a delivery problem.

Target audience (the neglected niche)

  • Senior IT professionals (10+ years)
  • Remote employees servicing US/UK/EU clients
  • B2-C1 English level - but performance collapses under pressure
  • Cannot translate technical excellence into executive presence

Research foundation (one sentence reference)

This project builds on the same five senior IT interviews described in the Needs Analysis case study. The core insight from that research: high competence, low performance under social pressure. Where the Needs Analysis focused on identifying gaps, this case study shows how BLUF writing directly solves the most common pattern: missing bottom lines in email updates and executive summaries.

Design

60‑min diagnostic demo + 5‑lesson executive module

The free 60‑minute demo (rapport, needs analysis, vocabulary, role‑based discussion) served as both diagnostic and conversion tool. Based on the gap analysis, a 5‑lesson executive module was designed:
• Lesson 1 (Headline‑First / BLUF),
• Lesson 2 (Staying Centered Under Pressure),
• Lesson 3 (DESC script for tough conversations),
• Lesson 4 (Executive storytelling with PARB),
• Lesson 5 (High‑stakes behavioural interview questions).
Every scenario uses real incidents from the five interviews - no textbook examples.

Develop

Lesson 1: BLUF method + production‑grade AI coach (Botpress, custom system prompt)

Lesson 1 was built around the BLUF (Bottom Line Up Front) method. The learner practices identifying "circling" vs "headline‑first" answers and delivers a 60‑second CEO update.


Botpress — an AI agent platform that lets learners paste any draft and get structured feedback instantly, without waiting for a coach. Learning continues between sessions.


To extend learning beyond live sessions, I engineered a standalone AI coach inside Botpress using a single, deeply‑optimized system prompt. The prompt forces the LLM to output structured feedback across five vectors: scenario classification, headline assessment, tone analysis, clarity score, and a copy‑paste‑ready BLUF rewrite. Botpress gives learners unlimited, private practice without needing a human coach available 24/7.


The architecture uses cognitive anchoring (Chain‑of‑Thought) - the agent first prints a public breakdown of the user’s draft, then references that breakdown to guide tone and structure. Early aggressive‑constraint versions triggered platform safety flags; the final design replaces financial penalties with structural anchoring, achieving perfect compliance without sacrificing execution power. The agent was also subjected to adversarial testing (policy puppetry, multi‑step translation leaks, contextual hijacking) and successfully deflected all attacks, defaulting to a hardened security perimeter.

03 - Key design decisions
Decision 1

Free 60‑min diagnostic demo (not a sales call)

Instead of a paid trial, the demo is a structured diagnostic. The trainer observes three tiers: casual conversation, vocabulary use, and role‑based professional discussion.

Decision 2

5‑lesson executive module built on real incidents

Every lesson uses anonymised incidents from the five senior IT interviews. Learners recognise themselves immediately - relatability drives engagement. Lesson 1 focuses entirely on BLUF: headline first, detail second.

Decision 3

Botpress AI coach with cognitive anchoring (no aggressive constraints)

Replaced earlier Coze prototypes (blocked by platform safety filters) with Botpress - a highly lenient prompt parsing layer that supports zero‑auth, shareable deployment. The result: a production‑ready coach that learners can use without login.

06 - Reflection

What worked

The free 60‑min diagnostic demo remains the right structure - it builds trust while providing a baseline before any payment. The BLUF method (Lesson 1) directly addresses a clear pattern from the interviews: professionals tend to circle around their point instead of leading with it. Moving from Coze (where aggressive constraint prompts triggered platform safety filters) to Botpress solved the compliance issue without sacrificing tone precision. Adversarial testing confirmed the agent’s security boundary: it deflected policy puppetry, multi‑step translation leaks, and contextual hijacking.

What I'd do differently

Create a lightweight self‑assessment version of the diagnostic simulation for leads who are not ready to commit to a full demo. Also integrate the Botpress coach directly into a Slack bot so learners can get feedback without leaving their workflow. Finally, I would like to build a live simulation version of the BLUF Coach using a platform like Mizou to evaluate real‑time spoken or typed responses – but that would require substantial testing to ensure reliability and pedagogical safety. That's why I opted for the asynchronous Botpress coach for now: it delivers immediate, structured feedback without the complexity of live scenario branching.
